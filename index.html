// Mathematical Core

AQMath calculations run client-side with local portfolio data, combining Risk Parity, KKT projection, volatility-based allocation caps, full Covariance, and Variance Matrix analysis for privacy-preserving portfolio risk control. The engine treats volatility as a constraint, not decoration: high-variance assets receive smaller dynamic caps, correlated assets are penalized through covariance, and underweight assets are prioritized only when they improve the risk-balanced structure.

Risk Model
Full covariance matrix estimates how assets move together, then Risk Parity targets equalized risk contribution.
Optimization
KKT-style projection keeps allocations inside volatility caps and prevents oversized high-risk positions.
Privacy
Portfolio amounts stay in local browser storage; server calls receive only symbols needed for market data.

// Frequently Asked Questions

How does the engine calculate risk?
The Pro engine uses a Variance Matrix, asset Covariance, volatility estimates, and KKT-style projection to calculate Risk Parity allocations where each asset contributes a controlled share of portfolio risk.
How is AQMath actually private?
Your portfolio amounts, entry prices, and allocation choices remain in your browser. AQMath uses client-side local storage and does not collect personal data or store your portfolio on a server.
Is the free tier a real tool or just a demo?
The free version is fully functional for manual portfolio tracking, CSV-based historical prices, DCA distribution, and browser-local export/import. Pro adds automated historical data and full Risk Parity optimization.
Why are market widgets separated from features?
Live markets are informational context: ticker data, Fear & Greed, BTC dominance, movers, and exchange status. They support decision-making but are intentionally separate from the product feature story.
AQMath does not provide financial advice. All calculations are based on historical data and mathematical models.
All calculations are performed client-side; no personal data is collected.

❓ FAQ
What is AQMath?
A serverless tool for long-term passive crypto portfolio management using DCA and quantitative optimization (Risk Parity).
How does DCA distribution work?
You enter a total budget, and the app allocates it to tokens below their target allocation, considering risk and average cost.
What does the Quantum Engine do?
It uses 180 days of price history to compute optimal allocation weights (Risk Parity) so each token contributes equally to portfolio risk.
How to import data?
You can manually enter, import a CSV with transactions (Date, Type, Symbol, Amount) or load a previously exported JSON.
What do ❄️ / 🔥 mean?
Freeze (❄️) excludes the token from DCA and optimization; useful for positions you don't want to change.
Why the ⚠️ warning?
The token has less than 180 days of price history – optimization results are less reliable.
Is my data safe?
Everything stays in your browser (localStorage). No server, no data transmission.
