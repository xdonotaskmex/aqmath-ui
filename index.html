// Frequently Asked Questions

How is AQMath actually private?
Most tools claim privacy but still send your data to a server. AQMath is different by architecture: your portfolio (amounts, values, entry prices) never leaves your browser. The engine receives only token symbols – it has no idea how much of anything you hold. Everything is stored in localStorage. No accounts, no cookies, no tracking.
What makes the math institutional-grade?
This is not a fixed-percentage calculator. AQMath uses:
  • Risk Parity with full covariance matrix – not simple equal-weight
  • Projected Gradient Descent – the same algorithm class used by hedge funds
  • Iterative KKT projection – guarantees no token exceeds its volatility limit
  • Dynamic max weights derived from volatility – more volatile tokens get smaller caps
This is a production mathematical engine, not an Excel spreadsheet.
Why are the Engine and DCA separated?
Other tools merge optimization and execution into one function. AQMath keeps them completely independent: the Engine calculates optimal targets (Risk Parity strategy), while the DCA executes purchases with safety filters (volatility, trend, self-sustainability). Neither depends on the other – each can be improved independently.
What is the APY self-sustainability filter?
Tokens with high staking yield (APY ≥ 10%) are marked as self-sustaining. This information flows into the DCA engine as a safety factor — higher yield means lower historical volatility risk. Unlike simple skip-logic found elsewhere, AQMath integrates APY into the mathematical weight calculation:
  • Yield-bearing tokens still receive DCA if underweight
  • Their self-sustaining status adjusts volatility weighting
  • The Engine periodically rebalances the entire portfolio
Nobody else has this “yield farming” logic built into DCA.
Why a microservice architecture?
AQMath is split into 6 independent services: Collectors → Pipeline → Database → Engine / DCA → UI. Each has its own repository, responsibility, and deployment. This enables scaling, easy maintenance, and future NFT-gated protection – unlike monolithic projects that are hard to extend.
Is the free tier a real tool or just a demo?
The free version is fully functional, not a trial. It includes:
  • Manual CSV upload for historical prices
  • Direct Binance SYNC for live prices
  • Full DCA distribution via Railway engine
  • Up to 4 tokens
Pro adds the Quantum Engine and automation – but the free tier is a complete, usable tool on its own.
No sign-up, no database – how does that work?
Everything you do stays local. The server exists only to compute math and return numbers. There is no user table, no login, no session cookies. GDPR-compliant by design, not as an afterthought. You own your data because it never leaves your machine.

❓ FAQ
What is AQMath?
A serverless tool for long-term passive crypto portfolio management using DCA and quantitative optimization (Risk Parity).
How does DCA distribution work?
You enter a total budget, and the app allocates it to tokens below their target allocation, considering risk and average cost.
What does the Quantum Engine do?
It uses 180 days of price history to compute optimal allocation weights (Risk Parity) so each token contributes equally to portfolio risk.
How to import data?
You can manually enter, import a CSV with transactions (Date, Type, Symbol, Amount) or load a previously exported JSON.
What do ❄️ / 🔥 mean?
Freeze (❄️) excludes the token from DCA and optimization; useful for positions you don't want to change.
Why the ⚠️ warning?
The token has less than 180 days of price history – optimization results are less reliable.
Is my data safe?
Everything stays in your browser (localStorage). No server, no data transmission.
